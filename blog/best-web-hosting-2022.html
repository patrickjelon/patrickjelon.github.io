Best Web Hosting 2022 (Great Deals)

Home/Blog/Best Web Hosting for 2022

If you own a business, regardless of the size, web hosting is no longer an option; it`s a necessity. Online searching is the norm nowadays, so your business needs a reliable and accessible website for potential clients to find. Gone are the days when businesses were listed in yellow pages; if you can`t be found in a search engine, your business doesn`t exist. Businesses need a sharable website to build an online presence. Without a website, your business lacks discoverability, and will earn little money. Granted, web hosting isn`t exclusively a business investment; if you`re aiming to host a personal site, blog, or project, hosting services are also highly valuable.

In order to help you find the best web hosting for your needs we have compiled the top 4 providers. Our developers with years of expreience have tried each one and listed their pros and cons. Each of these option have their advantages and are all excellent choices. Click the links below to find great deals!

#1. Bluehost

Bluehost makes sense for most small and medium businesses searching for hosting because of its managed WordPress hosting options and excellent 24x7 live chat assistance from seasoned specialists. Most businesses will also benefit from Blue Sky, an add-on service that offers specialised assistance in SEO, mobile optimization, and other areas to help you get the most out of your WordPress installation. Bluehost's shared hosting plans are all run on AMD servers with 2GB of RAM. You receive at least 50GB of storage space and a variety of functions, while automatic backups are only available on premium subscriptions. You'll love the point-and-click website builder if you need assistance putting your website together.


Pros

Feature rich plans, 30 days money back guarantee, Easy and intuitive management interface, 24/7 live chat is helpful

Cons

No monthly billing option (minimum 1 year), Only Linux based hosting, VPS and Dedicated plan lack customization

#2. HostGator

When it comes to shared hosting services, HostGator is the finest since it is dependable, the pricing is reasonable, and the firm promises a 99.9% uptime. Starting with the Hatchling subscription, which includes unmetered bandwidth, free WordPress/cPanel website transfer, and a free SSL certificate, the web hosting service provides three different shared hosting plans. The allocations are considerable, and should readily enough for a single domain's needs. HostGator offers a wide range of hosting services, from domains to dedicated servers, in addition to shared hosting. HostGator also offers WordPress hosting and, like Bluehost, has its own website builder, which is becoming increasingly widespread among web hosting businesses.


Pros

Free site migration, 45 days money back guarantee, Basic website builder included in shared plans cPanel, 24/7 support via phone and live chat

Cons

No free backups, Dedicated/VPS plans very costly, 99.9% uptime guarantee only on cloud hosting plans, No CDN

#3. Hostinger

Hostinger is the leader in cloud and premium shared hosting due to its speed, power, and affordability. Cloud Startup, Cloud Professional, and Cloud Enterprise are the three options offered for its cloud service, each of which includes 300 websites and unlimited bandwidth for your account. The Cloud Startup plan includes 200GB of SSD storage, 3GB of RAM, and two processor cores. All options include a free SSL certificate as well as a domain name. All plans include a free SSL certificate, free domain, daily backups, and the ability to host up to 300 websites, as well as a credit for Google Ads.


Pros

Affordable plans, Optimized for WordPress, SSD storage on all plans, 24/7 chat support, Weekly backup even in cheapest plan

Cons

No phone support, Doesn`t offer dedicated hosting, VPS plans not customizable, Daily backups, CDN, SSH access only in pricier plans

#4. SiteGround

SiteGround comes with a slightly more premium price tag, but it provides a solid offering with considerably more capabilities than you may imagine. The Startup plan - $6.99 per month for the first month, $14.99 per month after that - has 10GB of storage but includes unlimited bandwidth, sub and parked domains, email accounts, and databases. Easy installation of WordPress and other programmes, a free version of Weebly's website builder, and a free SSL certificate to safeguard your site are all available. The subscription also includes an integrated Cloudflare CDN, spam filtering from SPAM Experts, and automated daily backups, all of which are often costly extras elsewhere.


Pros

Feature-packed products, Five data center locations, Speedy support response times, Some unusual control panel extras

Cons

Above-average prices, Non-standard control panel, Limited storage space, No free domain
